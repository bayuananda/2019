Back to 2019

By Bayu Ananda

This is a day when the sound of firecrackers is everywhere, smell of grilled corn, sausage and meat everywhere and family, friends or couples are gather. Do you know what day it is? Yups, new year. New year is the day where we will start a new chapter. Live the new year with a glimmer of new hopes and dreams.

However, these 2 years the New Year's moment is a bit different. It's been two years since the COVID-19 pandemic is in our midst. Where everything is restricted, social, closure of tourist attractions, and much more. So we have to go back to the previous two years. Of course, we are back in 2019 the year when the pandemic has not yet entered our country.

31th december 2019 at 09:00 AM this story will start. That morning I got up from my sleep, knowing today is a day off from work. As usual, I went straight to the toilet to call nature while looking at the update on my cell phone. I saw a notification from the whatsapp groupâ€œTahun baru masa gak kuy? kuylah masa gak ngumpul nih" I was suddenly challenged to join. However, I was a little wondering if the rest of my friends would all gather? because we were already working at that time. I'm in Tangerang, the gathering place in Sukabumi, while the others are in Bandung, Yogyakarta, and Jakarta.

The past is the most beautiful memory, the present is the real one, the future is the hope of every human being. Hopefully all dreams can come true.

But if I think of it, the trip to there takes at least 5 hours, not include the traffic jams on holidays. Should I go or not? My daydream was interrupted when a notification came in. One of my friends shared his location on his way to the gathering place. Suddenly without thinking I immediately rushed to get ready to leave. As previously feared, of course the streets were crowded. Then I chose to use the commuter line. From the estimated 5 hours of travel, it turned out that I spent almost 9 hours.
